# Geological Formations & Tactical Implications Guide

## Overview

The tactical map generation system uses realistic geological formations to create terrain with authentic features. Each rock type creates specific terrain features that have tactical implications for tabletop gaming.

## Formation Types & Tactical Properties

### üèîÔ∏è Carbonate Formations (Karst Terrain)

#### LIMESTONE_KARST
- **Rock Type**: Carbonate (Calcite/Dolomite)
- **Hardness**: 3 (Soft)
- **Permeability**: Moderate
- **Weathering**: Chemical dissolution

**Terrain Features**:
- **Towers**: Isolated rock pillars (3/4 cover, high ground advantage)
- **Sinkholes**: Sudden depressions (hazard level 5, movement penalty)
- **Caves**: Underground passages (total concealment, ambush potential)
- **Karren**: Razor-sharp rock ridges (difficult terrain, 2x movement cost)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Excellent for ambushes and guerrilla warfare
- Natural defensive positions in towers
- Hidden movement through cave systems
- Dangerous terrain with sinkhole hazards

#### DOLOMITE_TOWERS
- **Rock Type**: Carbonate (Dolomite dominant)
- **Hardness**: 4 (Moderate)
- **Permeability**: Low
- **Weathering**: Slow chemical

**Terrain Features**:
- **Towers**: More resistant pillars (total cover, defensible)
- **Ledges**: Natural firing platforms (half cover, elevated position)
- **Cliffs**: Vertical barriers (impassable, line of sight blocker)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Superior defensive positions
- Natural sniper nests on ledges
- Channelizes movement around cliffs
- Towers provide strongpoints

### ‚õ∞Ô∏è Granitic Formations (Dome & Boulder Terrain)

#### GRANITE_DOME
- **Rock Type**: Granitic (Quartz/Feldspar/Mica)
- **Hardness**: 6 (Hard)
- **Permeability**: Low
- **Weathering**: Mechanical exfoliation

**Terrain Features**:
- **Domes**: Smooth rock hills (high ground, clear fields of fire)
- **Corestones**: Rounded boulders (half cover, scattered)
- **Grus**: Decomposed granite sand (difficult terrain in wet conditions)
- **Tors**: Boulder stacks (3/4 cover, climbing required)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Excellent observation posts on domes
- Boulder fields provide mobile cover
- Limited concealment (sparse vegetation)
- Good for ranged combat

#### WEATHERED_GRANODIORITE
- **Rock Type**: Granitic (Feldspar/Quartz/Hornblende)
- **Hardness**: 5 (Moderate-Hard)
- **Permeability**: Moderate
- **Weathering**: Combined mechanical/chemical

**Terrain Features**:
- **Corestones**: Abundant boulders (widespread cover)
- **Ravines**: Deep channels (concealed movement, chokepoints)
- **Grus**: Deep weathered soil (supports vegetation)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Good balance of cover and concealment
- Ravines create natural kill zones
- Supports forests for concealment
- Mixed terrain favors combined arms

### üåã Volcanic Formations (Columnar & Flow Terrain)

#### BASALT_COLUMNS
- **Rock Type**: Volcanic (Basalt)
- **Hardness**: 6 (Hard)
- **Permeability**: Low
- **Weathering**: Slow mechanical

**Terrain Features**:
- **Columns**: Hexagonal pillars (total cover, narrow passages)
- **Talus**: Rock debris slopes (difficult terrain, unstable)
- **Cliffs**: Columnar cliff faces (vertical barriers)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Maze-like terrain between columns
- Excellent for close-quarters combat
- Limited fields of fire
- Natural fortifications

#### VOLCANIC_TUFF
- **Rock Type**: Volcanic (Ash/Clay)
- **Hardness**: 2 (Very soft)
- **Permeability**: High
- **Weathering**: Rapid erosion

**Terrain Features**:
- **Hoodoos**: Mushroom rocks (unique cover shapes)
- **Alcoves**: Eroded shelters (concealment, protected positions)
- **Tuff**: Soft ground (easy to dig, temporary fortifications)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Easily modified terrain (trenches, foxholes)
- Natural shelters in alcoves
- Rapid erosion creates changing battlefield
- Poor foundation for structures

### üèúÔ∏è Clastic Formations (Canyon & Fin Terrain)

#### SANDSTONE_FINS
- **Rock Type**: Clastic (Quartz/Clay)
- **Hardness**: 5 (Moderate)
- **Permeability**: High
- **Weathering**: Mechanical (wind/water)

**Terrain Features**:
- **Fins**: Narrow rock walls (channelized movement, ambush sites)
- **Slot Canyons**: Very narrow passages (single-file movement, chokepoints)
- **Alcoves**: Wind-carved shelters (concealment, camp sites)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Extreme chokepoints in slot canyons
- Parallel fins create corridors
- Limited lateral movement
- Excellent for delaying actions

#### CROSS_BEDDED_SANDSTONE
- **Rock Type**: Clastic (Pure Quartz)
- **Hardness**: 4 (Moderate-Soft)
- **Permeability**: High
- **Weathering**: Differential erosion

**Terrain Features**:
- **Hoodoos**: Balanced rocks (unique landmarks, minor cover)
- **Alcoves**: Layered shelters (tiered defensive positions)
- **Fins**: Cross-bedded walls (climbable with ledges)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Multiple elevation levels for combat
- Natural climbing routes
- Good observation points
- Distinctive landmarks for navigation

### ü™® Metamorphic Formations (Folded & Platy Terrain)

#### FOLIATED_SCHIST
- **Rock Type**: Metamorphic (Mica/Quartz)
- **Hardness**: 4 (Moderate)
- **Permeability**: Low
- **Weathering**: Along foliation planes

**Terrain Features**:
- **Foliation Planes**: Parallel ridges (linear cover, predictable pattern)
- **Schist Ravines**: Folded channels (complex terrain, multiple routes)
- **Crenulations**: Wavy rock patterns (irregular cover)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Complex terrain with multiple paths
- Natural defensive lines along foliation
- Difficult to navigate
- Good for defensive operations

#### SLATE_BEDS
- **Rock Type**: Metamorphic (Clay/Mica)
- **Hardness**: 3 (Soft)
- **Permeability**: Impermeable
- **Weathering**: Mechanical splitting

**Terrain Features**:
- **Foliation Planes**: Flat splitting surfaces (natural pavements)
- **Talus**: Slate fragments (difficult terrain, noisy movement)
- **Ledges**: Stair-step terrain (tiered positions)

**Tactical Implications**:
- Noisy movement over talus (stealth penalty)
- Natural stepped defensive positions
- Impermeable surface creates water features
- Flat areas good for artillery

## Integration with Game Systems

### D&D 5th Edition
- Use cover rules as written
- Difficult terrain costs 2x movement
- Climbing DCs based on rock hardness (DC 10-20)
- Perception checks modified by terrain features

### Pathfinder
- Apply terrain modifiers to Acrobatics and Stealth
- Use hazard rules for sinkholes and unstable ground
- Natural fortifications provide circumstance bonuses

### Generic Systems
- Movement penalties for rough terrain
- Cover bonuses for defensive positions
- Elevation advantages for ranged combat
- Concealment for stealth operations

## Conclusion

The geological formation system creates realistic, tactically interesting terrain based on real-world geology. Each formation type offers unique tactical opportunities and challenges, encouraging players to adapt their strategies to the terrain rather than treating it as mere scenery.
